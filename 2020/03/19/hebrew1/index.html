Once again with English translation.

In Hebrew, when you want to talk about something that people do in general or always, u can start with “they are” or “they do” and what’s fun is that u should drop the subject “they”.

You begin with the word for “I,” ani , and you follow it with your name. You may notice that there is no word here for “am.” In Hebrew, the verb “to be” is not present in sentences in the present tense; it is implied. So when you say “I” in Hebrew, the “am” is implied.
Me’od means “very.” It often comes after the word that it modifies.s
You may have noticed that the adjective placement comes after the noun. This is different from English or Chinese where the adjective comes before the noun.
