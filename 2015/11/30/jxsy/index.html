6.4 Hardness measurement

Brinell Scale HB S/W Rockwell ScaleHR B/C/A, hard to soft
Soft : low-carbon steel,non-ferrous alloy
Hardened steel sphere/ alloy sphere
$HB=0.102\dfrac{F}{S}$		 120 degree diamond spheroconical : C/A
steel sphere : B

Step1

  1. find the head and lasting time according to estimating hardness

  2. turn on, set time by the screw

  3. turn hand wheel till head against the surface and wheel slippe

  4. push load button and screw the screw to turn on the timer

  5. measurement the diameter, get hardness from table

$HBS=\dfrac{F}{\pi Dh}=\dfrac{2F}{\pi D(D-\sqrt{D^2-d^2})}$

Step2

  1. find head by the estimating hardness

  2. turn hand wheel till head and sample touch,dial point to number 3, initial pressure 10kgf

  3. get the outside dial turn to 0

  4. turn load wheel to pointer to the wanting number 3-5 s, then remove the load

  5. read the number, outside for HRA HRC, inside for HRB

7.3.1 Surface Roughness Measurement

K the gain

$R_z=i\dfrac{|(h_1+h_2+\cdots+h_5)-(h_6+h_7+\cdots+h_{10})|}{5} {\rm \mu m}$

$\overline{R_z}=\dfrac{R_{z1}+R_{z2}+\cdots+R_{zn}}{n}{\rm \mu m}$

Step

  1. estimate the tested sample with the model, decide the sample length and judge length
  2. turn on the microscope
  3. coarse justify the focal length
    1. loose 3
    2. screw down 6 to down place
    3. screw up till green band in the eyepiece
  4. fine justify 14 near the eyepiece
  5. loose screw at eyepiece turn to one line parallel to the light band

7.4 Screw Thread Measurement

Aim

  • Major diameter
  • Pitch
  • flank angle

Step

  1. just the focus, and so on of the instrument [expensive, only 2 of them, skip, justify by the teacher]

  2. flank angle

    1. screw the front and side to move the cross at mid of the tooth

    2. screw the wheel beside the eyepiece

    3. the A-A dotted line from vertical 0 degree to parallel close to the line of the tooth

    4. measure 4 place

      $\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(left)=\dfrac{\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(\rm I)+\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(IV)}{2}\\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(right)=\dfrac{\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(\rm II)+\dfrac{\alpha}{2}(III)}{2}$

    ERROR : $\Delta \dfrac{\alpha}{2}( \rm left)=\dfrac{\alpha}{2}( \rm left)-\dfrac{\alpha}{2}\\Delta \dfrac{\alpha}{2}( \rm right)=\dfrac{\alpha}{2}( \rm right)-\dfrac{\alpha}{2}$

  3. Major diameter

    1. let A-A half in the screw and A-A touch the line of tooth
    2. read the front wheel number
    3. move front wheel to II, get $d_2(left)$
    4. III, IV, get$d_2(right)$

    $d_2=\dfrac{d_2({\rm left})+d_2({\rm right})}{2}$

    $\Delta d_2=d_{2m}-d_2$

  4. Pitch

    1. A-A touch the line read the number of side wheel
    2. move to n pitch II, read side wheel
    3. same III, IV

    $P_{nm}=\dfrac{P_{n{\rm left}}+P_{n{\rm right}}}{2}$

    $\Delta P_n=P_{nm}-P_n$

7.5.5 Gear base tangent length deviation measurement

Pitch Unevenness

Step

  1. calculate $W=m[1.476\times(2n-1)+0.014z], n={\rm round}(\dfrac{z}{9}+\dfrac{1}{2})$
  2. just to 0
  3. measure around the gear

length variation : $\Delta F_W=W_{max}-W_{min}$

average length error : $\Delta E_W=W_{av}-W=\dfrac{\sum\limits^z_1 W_i}{z}-W$

Satisfaction $\Delta F_W\le F_W\E_{Wi}\le\Delta E_W\le E_{Ws}\E_{Ws}=E_{ss}\cos\alpha-0.72F_r\sin\alpha\E_{Wi}=E_{si}\cos\alpha+0.72F_r\sin\alpha$

  • $E_{ss}$ : tooth thickness upper dievation
  • $E_{si}$ : tooth thickness lower dievation
  • $F_r$ : Radial runout tolerance

9.3 Belt Drive

Slip ratio

$\varepsilon=\dfrac{v_1-v_2}{v_1}=\dfrac{d_1n_1-d_2n_2}{d_1n_1}$

Effectiveness

$\eta=\dfrac{P_2}{P_1}=\dfrac{T_2n_2}{T_1n_1}$

Step

  1. Set initial force $F_0$
  2. turn off the velocity knob, then turn on power, clean, and set to rotating speed to 1200$\pm$5 r/min
  3. record the rotating speed and torque of the forced wheel with no load
  4. then add load, just the rotating speed of the major, record
  5. do 8 times
  6. close all, clean all
  7. draw $\varepsilon -T_2$, $\eta- T_2$ graphs

9.5.1 Closed gear

$P_1=P_9-P_9\times\eta_{sum}$

Effectiveness

$\eta=\sqrt{\dfrac{T_9-T_1}{T_9}}$

Transmission ratio=1

T9

$T=\frac{WL}{2}=T_9$

Step

  1. turn down knob to 0, turn on power
  2. clean all
  3. set rotating speed to 300-800r/min
  4. Weight on the plate, record
  5. then next, record till 8 of weight
  6. clean all, close all
  7. draw $\eta-T_9$ and $T_1-T_9$
